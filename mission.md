# Core mission

The network is a peer-led, globally inclusive consortium serving as a committee,
forum, and collective voice to advocate, educate, and set standards for open,
reproducible and reliable research practices in computational biology,
biomedical informatics and related fields.

## Background

Reproducibility in bioinformatics and computational biology has been neglected
for a long time.
Although the tools and capabilities exist to enable 100% reproducibility and
transparency in computational research, many research outputs in our field
have problems.
Research that is irreproducible cannot be audited, and may be hiding more
substantial problems, such as invalid methods or inconsistencies with published
methods.
Increasing the reliability of published research is a key goal of the network,
and computational reproducibility is an important first step in doing that.
Irreproducible computational research is a major cause of research waste,
and adopting reproducible, open science practices encourages reuse, increasing
the positive impact of the work.
Computational reproducibility encourages peers to cross-check one anothers work
and supports the self-correcting nature of science.
Urgent action is required to improve the quality of computational biology
research practices, which will prevent waste and increase trust in the work
done by researchers.

## Our aims and scope

We will address these problems with all available approaches including
education, meta-research, recommendations and policy initiatives.
A key role of the network is to raise awareness to these issues to various
stakeholders including the research community, policy makers and the general
public.
The RN provides a platform for leaders in the field to come together to
formulate solutions to the reproducibility problems we face in computational
biology.
The network aims to be as inclusive as possible, encompassing the fields of 
computational biology, biomedical informatics, cheminformatics, neuroinformatics
and data-intensive medical research and psychology.
We want to include researchers and practitioners at all career levels, from
undergraduate students, doctoral students, research assistants, post-docs and
senior scientists in the research and industry sectors.
As the reproducibility problems we face are caused by a multitude of factors,
the network will involve experts in training and education, publishing,
research integrity, computational research practices, domain expertise, 
meta-research, policy and funding.
We will work closely with related reproducibility groups and organisations
representing computational biology and related fields.

## Our activities

The network will be involved in the following activities to further the mission:

* Host educational events and produce materials including workshops and tutorials.
* Developing best practice guidelines, recommendations and policy.
* Media and online presence incl newsletter, social media and website
* Funding initiatives
* Awards for excellence
* Career development opportunities
* Cooperation with other bodies
* Events (journal club, seminar, online symposium, etc)

## Our partners

We are looking to partner with like-minded organisations to support some of these
activities.
In particular, we are seeking funding support for awards, seed grants and events.
