# Core mission

The network is a peer-led, globally inclusive consortium serving as a committee,
forum, and collective voice to advocate, educate, and set standards for open,
reproducible and reliable research practices in computational biology,
biomedical informatics and related fields.

## The pitch

Reproducibility in bioinformatics and computational biology has been neglected
for a long time.
Although the tools and capabilities exist to enable 100% reproducibility and
transparency in computational research, many research outputs in our field
have problems.
We will address these problems with a combination of education, recommendations
and policy initiatives.
Urgent action is required now to improve the quality of computational biology
research practices, which will prevent waste and increase trust in the work
done by researchers.
The RN provides a platform for leaders in the field to come together to
formulate solutions to the reproducibility problems we face in computational
biology.

## Our scope

The network aims to be as inclusive as possible, encompassing the fields of 
computational biology, biomedical informatics, cheminformatics, neuroinformatics
and data-intensive medical research and psychology.

We want to include researchers and practitioners at all career levels, from
undergraduate students, doctoral students, research assistants, post-docs and
senior scientists in the research and industry sectors.

## Our activities

* Educational events and materials including workshops and tutorials.
* Developing best practices, recommendations and policy
* Media and online presence incl newsletter, social media and website
* Funding initiatives
* Awards for excellence
* Career development opportunities
* Cooperation with other bodies
* Events (journal club, seminar, online symposium, etc)

## Our partners

We are looking to partner with like-minded organisations to support some of these
activities.
In particular, we are seeking funding support for awards, seed grants and events.
